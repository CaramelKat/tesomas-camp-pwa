Adult Leader Training's

  • Youth Protection Training

    Sunday: Youth Protection Training is required to all adults prior to attending camp. Training is available at my.scouting.org. If you come without training, you will be required to complete the training on Sunday before dinner.

    Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills

    Monday - Friday, 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM, Eagle Quest: Designed to sharpen camping and other outdoor skills so that leaders can deliver the outdoor portion of the Scouting program. Is required for new Scoutmasters and assistants to be considered “Trained”. The course prepares leaders to teach the outdoor skills that Scouts need for Tenderfoot, Second Class, and First Class. This course is also highly recommended for troop committee members, Webelos leaders, and leaders of venturing units.
    Must Attend All Days

    Paddle Craft Safety

    Monday - Friday, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM, Tesomas Waterfront: This BSA training expands Safety Afloat, as well as the knowledge, needed for a unit leader to confidently supervise canoeing or kayaking excursions on flat water.
    Must Attend All Days

    Swimming and Water Rescue

    Monday - Friday, 10:30 AM - 12:00 PM, Tesomas Waterfront: Going beyond Safe Swim Defense, this training will provide leaders with information and skills to prevent, recognize, and respond to swimming emergencies during unit swimming activities.

    Adult C.O.P.E.

    Monday - Friday, 2:00 PM-3:30 PM, C.O.P.E. and Climbing: Learn fun games an activities that you can share with your scouts or use to run a more engaging and entertaining troop meeting. Don’t be confused by having an intense amount of fun with adults from different troops. This Challenging Outdoor Personal experience is not all fun and games; it’s fun, games, and social.

    Safe Swim Defense & Safety Afloat

    Wednesday, 2:00 - 3:30 PM, Tesomas Waterfront: Learn what it takes to safely operate aquatic programs within your troop.
